# Industry-Specific AI Training Tracks

Welcome to the AI Academy Industry Tracks. These specialized modules help you apply your AI skills to real-world scenarios in your specific sector.

## Why Industry-Specific Training?

General AI skills are essential, but every industry has unique:

- **Use cases** that drive the most value
- **Regulations** governing AI usage
- **Workflows** where AI integrates
- **Ethics** considerations specific to your sector
- **Language** and terminology you need to master

## What Each Track Includes

Every industry track provides:

1. **Industry Overview** - Sector landscape and AI adoption state
2. **Use Case Library** - 20+ AI use cases ranked by impact
3. **Role-Specific Guides** - Prompt libraries for common roles
4. **Compliance & Ethics** - Regulatory requirements and ethical considerations
5. **Case Studies** - Real implementation examples with ROI
6. **Templates & Tools** - Ready-to-use prompts and workflows

## How to Choose Your Track

**If you work in multiple industries:** Start with your primary sector, then add tracks as needed. Many skills transfer between sectors.

**If your industry isn't listed:** Choose the closest match, then adapt the concepts. The Professional Services track is often a good general-purpose choice for knowledge work.

**If you're a consultant or vendor:** Consider multiple tracks to serve diverse clients effectively.

## Prerequisites

Before starting an industry track, you should have completed:

- AI Academy Level 1 (Student) - Core AI literacy
- OR equivalent practical experience with AI tools

The tracks assume you understand:
- Basic prompting (RTC framework)
- AI tool categories (chat, research, creation)
- AI safety and privacy fundamentals

## Certification

Complete any industry track to earn your **AI Academy - Industry Specialist** certification in that sector.

**Requirements:**
- Complete all track modules
- Pass the industry-specific assessment (70% threshold)
- Submit one implemented use case with documented results

**Validity:** 2 years (recertification via updated assessment)
