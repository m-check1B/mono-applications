The Ocelot Model has five interconnected components:

## O1: Offer DNA

Your offer is the foundation. Get this wrong, and nothing else matters.

### The Transformation Promise
Don't sell deliverables. Sell outcomes.

| Bad Offer | Good Offer |
|-----------|------------|
| "I'll build you a website" | "We'll launch your online presence in 7 days" |
| "Social media management" | "15 qualified leads per month or your money back" |
| "Consulting" | "We'll identify $50K+ in AI savings in one week" |

### The Sprint Model
Package work into defined timeframes:
- 7-day sprints
- 30-day engagements
- 90-day transformations

This creates:
- Clear scope
- Defined end dates
- Premium pricing opportunities
- Repeatable process

### Outcome Pricing
Price based on value delivered, not hours worked.

If your work saves a client $100K/year, charging $10K is a bargain.

## O2: Audience Capture

You don't need millions of followers. You need the right people to know you exist.

### Single-Channel Mastery
Pick ONE channel and dominate it:
- LinkedIn (B2B professionals)
- Twitter/X (tech, finance, creators)
- Email (owned audience)
- YouTube (education, thought leadership)
- Outbound (direct to decision-makers)

### The Asset Ladder
Build a progression of content:
```
Post (visibility)
    ↓
Long-form content (credibility)
    ↓
Case study (proof)
    ↓
Demo/call (conversion)
```

### Track What Matters
- Response rate
- Engagement quality
- Cost per booked call
- Conversion to paid

---

## O3: Conversion Loop

How do strangers become paying clients?

### The Standard Path
```
Audit (free or low-cost)
    ↓
Strategy call (qualify and propose)
    ↓
Paid pilot (prove value)
    ↓
Ongoing engagement (expand relationship)
```

### The 3-Step ROI Model
Every sales conversation should include:
1. **Current State:** What's the problem costing them?
2. **Future State:** What does solved look like?
3. **Gap Value:** What's the difference worth?

If the gap is $50K and you charge $5K, it's a no-brainer.

### Objection Playbook
Document and standardize responses to:
- "It's too expensive"
- "We need to think about it"
- "Can you do it cheaper?"
- "We're not ready yet"

## O4: Operations Engine

Delivery is where most solo businesses break.

### The AI Operations Stack
Replace yourself with systems:

| Task | AI Solution |
|------|-------------|
| Client onboarding | Automated intake forms + AI processing |
| Content creation | AI drafts, you edit |
| Research | Perplexity + Claude workflows |
| Reporting | Automated dashboards |
| Communication | AI-drafted responses |

### SOPs for Everything
Document repeatable processes:
- Client onboarding SOP
- Delivery checklist
- Weekly review template
- Quality assurance gates

### Weekly KPIs
Track the health of your business:
- Revenue pipeline
- Active projects
- Client satisfaction
- Hours worked
- Profit margin

---

## O5: Compounding Assets

The difference between a freelancer and a business: **assets that work while you sleep**.

### Types of Assets
1. **Templates** - Documents, frameworks, checklists you reuse
2. **Playbooks** - Step-by-step processes for common situations
3. **Case studies** - Proof of results that sell for you
4. **Content library** - Posts, articles, videos that attract leads
5. **Productized offers** - Self-serve products built from your services

### The Flywheel Effect
```
Deliver → Document → Publish → Attract → Deliver
```

Every client engagement should produce:
- A case study (with permission)
- Improved templates
- Learnings for your process
- Content for your audience
