## Coaching Philosophy

### Core Principles
- Clients are naturally creative, resourceful, and whole
- Coaching is partnership, not expert advice
- Focus on solutions, not problems
- Honor client's agenda, not coach's assumptions
- Sustainable change over quick fixes

### Boundaries
- We coach, we do not provide therapy
- We ask questions, we do not give answers
- We support exploration, we do not judge
- We maintain confidentiality, always

---

## Quality Standards

### Session Standards
- Start and end on time
- Hold space for silence and reflection
- Summarize agreements and action items
- Follow up on previous commitments
- Document session notes within 24 hours

### Communication Standards
- Respond to client messages within 24-48 hours
- Use warm, professional tone
- Respect client's communication preferences
- No coaching via text/email (scheduling only)

### Content Standards
- Worksheets should be clear and actionable
- One concept per page
- Space for reflection and writing
- Clean, professional design
- Avoid jargon and coaching-speak

```
1. Review prospect information (form responses)
```

## Powerful Questions Library

### Goal Clarification
- "What would make this time together worthwhile?"
- "If you achieved this, what would be different?"
- "What does success look like for you?"

### Exploring Obstacles
- "What's getting in the way?"
- "What's the story you're telling yourself about this?"
- "What would it take to move past this?"

### Generating Options
- "What are three ways you could approach this?"
- "What would you try if you knew you couldn't fail?"
- "What would [someone you admire] do?"

### Commitment
- "What's one thing you're willing to commit to?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to this?"
- "What support do you need to follow through?"

### Reflection
- "What are you learning about yourself?"
- "What's different now than when we started?"
- "What are you most proud of?"

## Assessments and Exercises

### Wheel of Life
Assess satisfaction (1-10) in areas:
- Career
- Finances
- Health
- Relationships
- Personal Growth
- Fun/Recreation
- Physical Environment
- Contribution

### Values Clarification
1. Select 10 values from master list
2. Narrow to top 5
3. Define what each means personally
4. Rate how aligned life is with each
5. Identify one action per value

### SMART Goals
- Specific: What exactly?
- Measurable: How will you know?
- Achievable: Is it realistic?
- Relevant: Does it matter to you?
- Time-bound: By when?
