## Critical Notices

### Confidentiality
All client information is protected by attorney-client privilege and work product doctrine. Never:
- Share client names outside the firm
- Use client-specific information for other matters
- Store sensitive data in unsecured locations

### Ethical Obligations
- Duty of competence (Rule 1.1)
- Duty of confidentiality (Rule 1.6)
- Conflict of interest checks required (Rule 1.7)
- No unauthorized practice in other jurisdictions

### AI Usage Disclaimer
When AI is used in legal work:
- AI output is a starting point, not final work product
- Attorney must review and verify all AI-generated content
- Do not input highly sensitive client data into AI systems
- Maintain human judgment on all legal conclusions

## Quality Standards

### Document Standards
- Firm letterhead for client-facing documents
- Proper citation format: [Bluebook / OSCOLA / local rules]
- Double-space for court filings (check local rules)
- 1-inch margins, Times New Roman 12pt (or court requirements)
- Page numbers on all documents >1 page
- Document version control (v1, v2, FINAL)

### Research Standards
- Primary sources over secondary
- Verify case law is still good law (check for reversal/overruling)
- Include jurisdiction-specific rules
- Note any split in authority
- Update research before filing

### Review Standards
- Spell check is minimum, not maximum
- Cite check all authorities
- Verify defined terms are consistent
- Cross-reference all exhibits
- Partner/senior review before delivery

## Common Tasks

### Contract Review
```
1. Identify parties and purpose
2. Review key terms:
   - Payment/price
   - Term and termination
   - Representations and warranties
   - Indemnification
   - Limitation of liability
   - Confidentiality
   - Governing law/jurisdiction
3. Flag unusual or concerning provisions
4. Compare to market standard
5. Prepare redline or comment summary
```

### Legal Research
```
1. Identify legal issue(s)
2. Determine jurisdiction
3. Search for:
   - Statutes/regulations
   - Case law
   - Secondary sources (treatises, law review)
4. Analyze applicability to facts
5. Document findings with citations
6. Identify counterarguments
```

### Document Drafting
```
1. Gather client requirements
2. Identify applicable template/precedent
3. Customize for specific matter
4. Internal review
5. Client review/negotiation
6. Final execution copy
```

### Due Diligence
```
1. Define scope and timeline
2. Create document request list
3. Review materials:
   - Corporate documents
   - Contracts
   - IP/assets
   - Litigation/disputes
   - Regulatory compliance
4. Prepare diligence report
5. Identify material issues
```

## Matter Workflow

When working on matters:

1. **Intake**
   - Conflict check completed
   - Engagement letter signed
   - Matter number assigned

2. **Folder structure**
   ```
   /matters/[client]/[matter-name]/
   ├── 01-admin/           # Engagement, billing, conflicts
   ├── 02-correspondence/  # Client/opposing counsel comms
   ├── 03-research/        # Memos, case law
   ├── 04-drafts/          # Working documents
   ├── 05-filed/           # Court filings
   ├── 06-discovery/       # Produced/received
   └── 07-final/           # Executed agreements
   ```

3. **Deadline tracking**
   - All deadlines in docket management system
   - Early warning for court deadlines
   - Client-agreed deadlines documented

## Citation Format

### Case Citations (Bluebook)
```
[Case Name], [Volume] [Reporter] [Page], [Pincite] ([Court] [Year])
Example: Smith v. Jones, 123 F.3d 456, 460 (2d Cir. 2020)
```

### Statutory Citations
```
[Title] [Code] § [Section] ([Year])
Example: 15 U.S.C. § 78j(b) (2018)
```

### Regulation Citations
```
[Title] [C.F.R.] § [Section] ([Year])
Example: 17 C.F.R. § 240.10b-5 (2020)
```
